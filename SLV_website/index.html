About

This is the official website for a senior game design project in development at UC: Santa Cruz.

Bonjour! Voila Croissant, a robot specially designed by the people of Neila 13 to learn and store languages. He was built in the distant future by a group that is fascinated with all things archaic, a fascination which has led them to specialize in the retrieval of curious information from the past. Croissant, named after the only French word to have made it to the year 4002, is almost ready to embark on his creation-mission: to return to the 19th century to covertly recover the dead language of French.

Say La Vie is an educational RPG currently in production at the University of California: Santa Cruz by a group of computer game design students in the senior program. Our goal is to create a town simulation game, similar to Animal Crossing, that teaches the player basic French by immersing them in a rural French town. The player, who controls Crossant, will be challenged to decode French words while exploring the town, interacting with villagers, and playing simple mini-games. We hope to create a game that familiarizes the player with simple French vocabulary, sentence structure, and phonology, while maintaining player engagement and providing a really fun gaming experience.
